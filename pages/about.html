About

I am a first-year doctoral student of Engineering Science at the University of Oxford in the research group of Professor Antonis Papachristodoulou. I have graduated from Imperial College London with MEng in Biomedical Engineering in 2019. My research interests lie in the fields of synthetic biology, systems biology, control engineering, and modelling of biological systems.

I believe that the applications of the engineering methods and utilization of the corresponding, results-focused mindset is the best way to address the major healthcare, economic, and environmental challenges of our times. And I think that engineering biology is also the best way to learn about living systems and to understand ourselves better. Because, as Richard Feynman expressed it nicely, "What I cannot create, I do not understand."

The specific engineering problems that I am passionate about are:

  • Design of the optimal controllers in the synthetic biomolecular feedback systems
  • Applications of Control Theory to the biological systems
  • Construction of synthetic cells

I will soon write more about these (bio)engineering challenges and I will explain why they matter. You can also read my Blog, where I share my thoughts, summarize what I learned from my experiences, and present some of my projects.

Throughout the course of my undergraduate studies at Imperial, I took part in several interesting engineering projects at Imperial College London, California Institute of Technology and Stanford University, either as part of my curriculum, through my extracurricular activities, or during the summer research internships. You can view some of these projects in the Projects section of this website.

Furthermore, I try to stay active in other, non-research fields. My activities include:

  • Promotion of bioengineering and synthetic biology in Slovakia through lecturing and podcast Pravidelna Davka

  • Mentoring of young students

  • Free assistance with the admissions processes to the World's top universities for the talented Slovak and Czech students in the non-profit organization Unimak

To see (somewhat) more comprehensive list of my activities, check my CV.

In addition to that, I like to drink good coffee, to have meaningful conversations about things that matter, lifting weights, cooking, and playing the accordion.
